# Privacy Regime Checks

Use this as a starter checklist only. Verify current official sources before making legal conclusions, citing thresholds, relying on deadlines, or deciding final obligations.

## GDPR / EU GDPR / UK GDPR-Style Review

Check:

- Territorial applicability.
- Role: controller, joint controller, processor, or subprocessor.
- Personal data and special-category data.
- Children or vulnerable people.
- Specific purposes.
- Lawful basis for each purpose.
- Consent: specific, granular, revocable, evidenced, and not dark-patterned.
- Legitimate interests: LIA purpose test, necessity test, balancing test, safeguards, and objection path.
- Privacy notices: complete, understandable, and aligned with actual processing.
- Data subject rights: access, erasure, rectification, portability, restriction, objection, consent withdrawal, and automated decision-making rights where applicable.
- Retention: defined, technically implemented, and supported across logs, backups, vendors, and archives.
- Processor contracts and subprocessor controls.
- Cross-border transfer mechanism.
- DPIA requirement or high-risk processing indicators.
- DPO involvement or requirement.
- Breach detection, assessment, escalation, and notification workflow.
- Accountability evidence retained.

## CCPA / CPRA-Style Review

Check:

- Applicability thresholds from current official sources.
- California resident or household relevance.
- Role: business, service provider, contractor, third party, or data broker.
- Notice at or before collection.
- Privacy policy coverage: personal information categories, purposes, sources, disclosures, sale/share, retention, rights, and request methods.
- Consumer rights: know/access, delete, correct, opt out of sale/sharing, limit sensitive personal information where applicable, and non-discrimination.
- "Do Not Sell or Share" and opt-out preference signal handling where required.
- Sensitive personal information identification and limitation where applicable.
- Service provider, contractor, and third-party contract terms.
- Data broker obligations where relevant.
- Risk assessment, cybersecurity audit, and automated decision-making technology triggers using current CPPA rules and guidance.
- Records of consumer requests and responses.

## Other Privacy Regimes

For LGPD, PIPEDA, other US state privacy laws, and sector-specific regimes, build a regime-specific checklist from official sources or user-provided legal materials.

Use these axes:

- Applicability.
- Definitions of personal and sensitive data.
- Controller/processor or equivalent roles.
- Legal basis or permitted purpose.
- Notice and transparency.
- Consent or opt-out.
- Individual rights.
- Children or vulnerable people.
- Automated decision-making or profiling.
- Data minimisation.
- Retention.
- Security safeguards.
- Vendor/processor contracts.
- Cross-border transfers.
- Breach notification.
- Recordkeeping and accountability.
- Enforcement risk.
