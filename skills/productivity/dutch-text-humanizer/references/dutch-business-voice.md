# Dutch Business Voice

Use this reference for Dutch business documents, especially strategy, tenders, ICT policy, operations, and client-facing proposals.

## Voice target

- Direct, zakelijke Nederlandse stijl.
- Confident but not boastful.
- Concrete before conceptual: numbers, clients, systems, responsibilities, and decisions carry the argument.
- Short to medium paragraphs. Avoid long chains with several `waarbij` or `zodat` clauses.
- Keep technical and product terms stable when they are part of the organization’s vocabulary.
- Use `we`, `VCS`, or the responsible team when the actor matters.

## VCS-style calibration

When sample documents from `Schrijfstijl en AI-voorbeeld/Schrijfstijl` are available, infer these defaults:

- VCS writes in practical business Dutch, often around service delivery, ICT, tenders, monitoring, maintenance, security, and operational continuity.
- The prose may use formal tender phrasing, but it is strongest when it explains what VCS does, how work is organized, and what the client receives.
- Keep company facts, client names, locations, product names, certifications, and technical terms intact.
- Reduce repeated self-praise. A claim like `toonaangevende integrator` is acceptable only if the surrounding text proves it with portfolio, customers, certifications, or scope.
- Prefer one clear sentence over a polished abstract paragraph.

## Rewrite moves

| AI-sounding Dutch | Better direction |
|---|---|
| `VCS speelt een cruciale rol in het snel veranderende beveiligingslandschap.` | `VCS beheert en integreert videomanagement- en toegangscontrolesystemen voor organisaties met complexe locaties.` |
| `Deze roadmap onderstreept onze ambitie om innovatie te versnellen.` | `Deze roadmap legt vast welke producten, markten en investeringen prioriteit krijgen.` |
| `De oplossing zorgt voor een naadloze gebruikerservaring.` | `Gebruikers melden zich via dezelfde interface aan en zien alleen de functies waarvoor zij rechten hebben.` |
| `Met een rijk portfolio aan diensten...` | `Het portfolio bestaat uit beheer, onderhoud, softwareontwikkeling, integratie en monitoring.` |
| `Dit vormt een belangrijke stap voorwaarts.` | `Hierdoor kan het team vanaf 2026 productontwikkeling en beheer apart sturen.` |

## Sentence-level guidance

- Use active voice when it clarifies ownership: `Het NOC monitort 24/7` instead of `Er wordt 24/7 gemonitord`.
- Keep passive voice when ownership is deliberately irrelevant or legally conventional.
- Replace vague benefits with operational consequences: lower downtime, clearer ownership, faster incident response, fewer handovers, better auditability.
- Break up stacked claims. If one sentence contains company history, market position, product portfolio, and strategy, split it.
- Avoid imported hype unless it is a product category: use `schaalbaar`, `betrouwbaar`, `beheerbaar`, `aantoonbaar`, and `meetbaar` only when the document explains how.

## DOCX-specific guidance

- Preserve headings, tables, page order, and paragraph count unless the user asks for a redesign.
- Prefer paragraph-level replacements. Do not collapse several paragraphs into one if the document layout depends on the original structure.
- Leave empty paragraphs and layout-only text untouched.
- Do not replace legal identifiers, dates, version labels, confidentiality labels, or table labels unless the user asks.
